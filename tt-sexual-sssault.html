What is Sexual Assault?

Sexual assault is any unwanted sexual act or behavior forced upon you without consent. It can occur in various contexts, including:

  • - Rape or Sexual Violence: Forced or coerced sexual acts.
  • - Sex Trafficking: Exploitation through coercion, manipulation, or force.
  • - IPSV (Intimate Partner Sexual Violence): Sexual violence within a romantic relationship.
  • - Sexual Harassment: Unwanted sexual advances or behaviors in the workplace, school, or other environments.

These experiences often leave lasting emotional and psychological scars, but you don’t have to carry them alone

The Impact
of Sexual Assault

Sexual assault doesn’t just affect you in the moment—it can have far-reaching effects
on your emotional, physical, and relational
well-being.

Survivors
may experience:

Emotional Struggles

Anxiety, depression, shame,
or guilt.

Difficulty Trusting

Challenges in forming or maintaining relationships, or even being in places that you previously considered safe

Triggers and Flashbacks

Reliving traumatic moments unexpectedly, or being ambushed by overwhelming emotions of fear, grief or anger

Physical Symptoms

Chronic pain, fatigue,
auto-immune disease or other stress-related conditions

You are not defined by what happened to you. With the right support, you can begin to heal and rebuild your life
