Understanding PTSD
in Service Professionals

PTSD often results from exposure to high-stress or life-threatening events, which are a regular part of life for first responders, law enforcement officers, and veterans. Common sources of trauma include:

  • - Combat and Military Service: Witnessing or experiencing life-threatening events.
  • - Emergency Response: Exposure to accidents, injuries, or suicide and fatalities as part of the job.
  • - Law Enforcement Work: Facing violence, hostility, or prolonged stress in dangerous situations.

These experiences can leave emotional artifacts that linger long after the events themselves, but healing is possible with the right support.

The Impact of PTSD

The effects of PTSD can extend beyond work
and affect all aspects of life.

You might notice:

Intrusive Memories

Flashbacks, nightmares, or feeling like the event is happening again

Hypervigilance

Feeling constantly on edge, irritable, or easily startled

Avoidance

Withdrawing from situations, people, or places that remind you of the trauma

Emotional Struggles

Anxiety, depression, or a sense of disconnection from loved ones

These symptoms don’t have to control your life. Therapy can provide the tools and support you need to heal and move forward.
