What is Childhood
Sexual Abuse Trauma?

Childhood sexual abuse can leave lasting emotional and psychological wounds that extend far beyond the initial event.

These experiences can profoundly disrupt how you see yourself, trust others, and connect with the world. However, with the right support, it’s possible to heal and rebuild.

The Impact
of Childhood Sexual Abuse

Trauma from childhood sexual abuse doesn’t simply fade with time—it often surfaces in subtle but impactful ways throughout your life.

You might notice:

Emotional Challenges

Feeling triggered or overwhelmed by small events

Relationship Struggles

Difficulty trusting others, setting boundaries, or enjoying
healthy intimacy

Behavioral Patterns

Avoidance, perfectionism, or self-sabotaging behaviors

Physical Symptoms

Chronic pain, fatigue, auto-immune disease or other stress-related conditions

These struggles do not define you. With trauma therapy, healing is not only possible but within reach.

How We Help

Our expert trauma therapy combines evidence-based techniques with a compassionate, personalized approach to help individuals and couples:

1

Reclaiming Your Story

Explore your past at your own pace, rebuilding self-trust and self-worth.

2

Processing the Trauma

Work through painful memories in a measured, safe manner using evidence-based methods.

3

Building Resilience

Develop tools to manage emotions, set healthy boundaries, and regain control of your life.

We specialize in advanced trauma recovery techniques, including EMDR, Internal Family Systems, Somatic Experiencing, and more. Each session is designed to help you feel safe, empowered, and supported.
